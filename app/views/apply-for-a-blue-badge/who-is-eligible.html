Who is eligible for a Blue Badge?

Blue Badges help people with disabilities or health conditions park closer to their destination. You can apply for a badge for yourself, somebody else or an organisation that transports people that need a Blue Badge.

In England, Scotland and Wales you can apply for a Blue Badge using the GOV.UK service. There's a different way to apply if you're in Northern Ireland.

If the applicant receives a benefit or is severely sight impaired

Somebody may automatically qualify for a Blue Badge if they either:

  • are severely sight impaired (blind)
  • received 8 or more points in the “moving around” part of the mobility assessment for Personal Independence Payment (PIP)
  • receive the higher rate of the mobility component for Disability Living Allowance (DLA)
  • receive the War Pensioners’ Mobility Supplement
  • receive a qualifying award under the Armed Forces Compensation Scheme

There is no automatic criteria for Attendance Allowance.

Automatic eligiblity varies slightly between England, Scotland and Wales.

Scotland and Wales

An applicant is automatically eligible if they scored 12 points in the “planning and following journeys” part of their mobility assessment for PIP.

Scotland

If an applicant previously received the higher rate of the mobility component for DLA indefinitely, they are eligible for a badge.

If an applicant has had a mandatory reconsideration for PIP accepted by the Department for Work and Pensions (DWP), they are eligible for a 1-year badge.

Wales

An applicant is automatically eligible if they receive tariff level 6 of the Armed Forces Compensation Scheme for a permanent mental disorder.

England

From September 2019, an applicant is automatically eligible if they scored 10 points in the “planning and following journeys” part of their mobility assessment for PIP. Specifically the 10 points which says “Cannot undertake any journey because it would cause overwhelming psychological distress to the claimant”.

If the applicant cannot walk or finds walking very difficult

An applicant may be eligible for a badge if one or more of the following applies:

  • they cannot walk at all
  • they cannot walk without help from someone else or using mobility aids
  • they find walking very difficult due to pain, breathlessness or the time it takes
  • walking is dangerous to their health and safety

If the applicant has a “hidden” or “non-physical” disability

In Scotland an applicant may be eligible for a badge if they a risk in traffic or in car parks. In Wales they may be eligible if they cannot plan or follow any journey without the help of someone else.

From September 2019 in England, an applicant may be eligible for a badge if they have a “hidden” or “non-physical” disability which causes them to severely struggle with journeys. We've written a page so that you can read more about the new criteria in England.

Other reasons for applying for a badge

An applicant may be eligible for a badge if they either:

  • have a terminal illness, which means they cannot walk or find walking very difficult and have a DS1500 form
  • have a severe disability in both arms and drive regularly, but cannot operate pay-and-display parking machines
  • are under the age of three and have a medical condition that means they always need to be accompanied by bulky medical equipment
  • are under the age of three and have a medical condition that means they must always be kept near a vehicle in case they need emergency medical treatment

Organisations

An organisation may be eligible for a badge (or multiple badges), if they:

  • care for, or assist people who need a Blue Badge
  • transport people who need a Blue Badge
