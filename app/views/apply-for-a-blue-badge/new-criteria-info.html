“Hidden” or “non-physical” disabilities or conditions

Before applying for a Blue Badge, check that you understand the new eligibility criteria.

To prepare for the new criteria, Department for Transport has done extensive research with an independent agency. As well as working with different specialists that care for people with various conditions and disabilities.

Due to the nature of “hidden” or “non-physical” disabilities, applications will be assessed by looking at an applicant's needs. Having a specific condition/disability does not automatically entitle somebody to a Blue Badge.

Applying with Personal Independence Payment (PIP)

An applicant is automatically eligible under the new criteria if:

  • they scored 10 points in “Planning and following a journey”
  • and only the 10 points which says “Cannot undertake any journey because it would cause overwhelming psychological distress to the claimant.”

If the applicant did not score this, they can still apply without PIP and their application will be assessed.

Applying without PIP

To help understand who might be eligible, consider applying for a Blue Badge if the applicant:

  • has a “hidden” or “non-physical” condition or disability, causing them to struggle with journeys
  • is a risk near vehicles, in traffic or car parks
  • struggles to plan or follow a journey
  • finds it difficult or impossible to control their actions and lacks awareness of the impact they could have on others
  • regularly has intense responses to overwhelming situations causing temporary loss of behavioural control (meltdown)
  • can become extremely anxious or fearful of public/open spaces

If one or more of these are relevant to the applicant, apply using the online service.
