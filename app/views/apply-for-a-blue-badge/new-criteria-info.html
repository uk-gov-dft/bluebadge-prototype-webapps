“Hidden” / “non-physical” disabilities or conditions

At the start of September 2019, English local authorities are introducing a new criteria for Blue Badge applications.

To prepare for the new criteria, Department for Transport (DfT) conducted extensive research with an independent agency. This research included insights from multiple different specialists that care for people with relevant conditions and disabilities as well as local authorities that issue the badges.

Due to the nature of “hidden” or “non-physical” disabilities, applications will be assessed by looking at an applicant's needs. Having a certain condition or disability does not automatically entitle somebody to a Blue Badge.

If the applicant is at least 16 years old and receives Personal Independence Payment (PIP)

An applicant could be automatically eligible under the new criteria if, in their PIP assessment:

  • they scored 10 points in “Planning and following a journey”
  • specifically the 10 points which says “Cannot undertake any journey because it would cause overwhelming psychological distress to the claimant.”

If the applicant did not score this, they can still apply without PIP and their application will be assessed by their local authority.

Other applicants

Consider applying for a Blue Badge if the applicant has a “hidden” / “non-physical” condition or disability, causing them to severely struggle with journeys and either:

  • is a risk near vehicles, in traffic or car parks
  • struggles to plan or follow a journey
  • finds it difficult or impossible to control their actions and lacks awareness of the impact they could have on others
  • regularly has intense responses to overwhelming situations causing temporary loss of behavioural control (meltdown)
  • can become extremely anxious or fearful of public/open spaces

If one or more of these are relevant to the applicant, apply using the online service.
