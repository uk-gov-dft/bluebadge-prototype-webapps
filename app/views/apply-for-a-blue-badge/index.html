Check eligibility and apply for a Blue Badge. You can also use this service to reapply for a Blue Badge.

You can apply for yourself, someone else or an organisation.

A Blue Badge costs up to £10 in England and £20 in Scotland. It’s free in Wales.

New criteria

You can now apply for a Blue Badge for somebody that struggles with journeys due to a “hidden” or “non-physical” condition or disability.

Before you apply, find out more about the new criteria.

What you’ll need

You’ll need a recent digital photo showing the applicant's head and shoulders.

You’ll also need a photo or scan of:

  • proof of identity (such as a birth certificate, passport or driving licence)
  • proof of address (such as a utility bill or government letter)
  • proof of benefit (if applicable)

You’ll also need:

  • the applicant's National Insurance number (if applicable)
  • the badge number (if you're reapplying for a Blue Badge)

There’s a different way to apply if you’re in Northern Ireland.

Before you start

You can also return to a saved Blue Badge application.

The application will be sent to the applicant's issuing local authority.- they’ll make a decision within 3 weeks on average, although this may take up to 12 weeks.

Renewing a Blue Badge

A Blue Badge usually lasts for 3 years. Before it expires, you’ll need to reapply for the badge to renew. This makes sure everybody that holds a Blue Badge genuinely needs one and all details are kept up to date.

Questions

For questions about the Blue Badge scheme, contact your local council.
